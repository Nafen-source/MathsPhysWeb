Shape Memory Alloys

Shape memory alloys exhibit an effect whereby, after being deformed, and once sufficient heat is applied, they return to their original undeformed shape. Some shape memory alloys have another effect called super elasticity where any bends in the material are immediately reversed.
There are many applications in engineering such as vibration dampers which are useful for noise reduction in aerospace engineering and reducing the effects of earthquakes on buildings in civil engineering. They are also used in flexible glasses frames and as a light-weight actuator in robotics.

The effect is due to phase transitions between high-temperature austenite which has only one type of unit cell and low-temperature martensite which has many types of unit cells. The deformation gradients of which are called the variants. The larger number of variants in martensite corresponds
to a breaking of the symmetry that the material enjoyed at higher temperatures. Nucleation causes these variants to collect into regions, that is it is energetically favourable to do so. Together these regions form a microstructure.
